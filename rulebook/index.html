# Military Context

# Commanders

The Toyota Wars is a two-player simulation of a series of military conflicts that played out between 1983-1987 in the country of Chad. These conflicts included an civil war, the overthrowing of Goukouni's government by commander Hissène Habré, and the invasion of the country by neighboring Libya under the leadership of Muammar Gaddafi.

The term "Toyota Wars," "Toyota War," or "Great Toyota War" is sometimes used to refer to portions of the Chadian civil war of 1982-87, and other times is used to refer to the final phase of the Chadian-Lybian conflict (opens new window), which lasted from 1978 to 1987. This game includes elements of both conflicts, but focuses primarily on the civil war between Hissène Habré (opens new window), Commander of FAN (Armed Forces of the North), and Goukouni Oueddei (opens new window), Commander of FAP (the People's Armed Forces). Each player controls one of these two commanders.

Lybia, France, and the OAU (the Organization for African Unity), may intervene and assist in various ways, but the larger political decisions on which intervention is based are not controlled by either player.

There are multiple possible scenarios to chose from when playing The Toyota Wars, each of them covering a specific period in the Chadian civil war, and, to a lesser extent, the Chadian-Lybian conflict.

  • This rulebook focuses on the Opération Manta scenario, which covers a two-year period in the war between 1983 and 1984. This period of the civil war is characterized by the simultaneous invasion of Chad by Libyian forces and France's attempt at preventing such an invasion from toppling Habré's government in N'Djaména.

    The goal of Gaddafi's invasion is to get Goukouni in power in order to augment his chances of controlling Chad once the civil war is over.

    France's goal is to maintain political stability no matter which government currently controls her former colony in order to continue benefiting from a privileged economic and military relationship with Chad. The French intervention under President François Mitterand was codenamed Opération Manta (opens new window), or "Operation Stingray."

In Opération Manta, Habré is the initial Government Commander. During the course of the game, Goukouni may become the government Commander by capturing the capital city, N'Djmanéna, with the help of the invading Lybians and allied guerrilla factions.

# Combat Units

Below are all combat units which both Commanders — and hence both players — have at their disposal.

# Conventional Units

# Conventional Government Allies
  • FANT Government army. Although recruited by Habré, these units come under Goukouni's control if he succeeds in taking control of the government.
  • MANTA French Marine and Air Force units from the Manta task force. Controlled by any government player.
  • OAU Peacekeeping forces from the Organization for African Unity. Controlled by any government player.
# Conventional FAP Allies
  • Task Force P Lybian troops, some of them from the Islamic Legion, sent by Gaddafi to aid his Chadian ally Goukouni.

# Guerrilla Units

In addition to FAN and FAP, there are a total of 12 other Guerrilla Factions participating in the civil war. Their allegiance to one of the two Commanders will vary throughout the game. Below are the guerrilla factions involved in Opération Manta.

# FAN and FAN-allied Guerrilla Allies
  • FAN Habré's personal army. Whether Habré is government commander or rebel commander, FAN remain loyal to him.
  • MPLT Allied rebel troops.
  • PLA Allied rebel troops.
# FAP and FAP-allied Guerrilla Allies
  • FAP Goukouni's personal army. Whether Goukouni is government commander or rebel commander, FAP remain loyal to him.
  • CDR Allied rebel troops.
  • CODOS Allied rebel troops.
# Neutral
  • MRP "Inactive" guerrilla units. These units do not engage in combat until their city of Moundou 1849is attacked. At this poing, they are activated and fight against the attacker.
    However, the Government Player can attempt to rally them with a roll of the dice at beginning of each Tactical Phase.

The single most important thing to remember about guerrilla units is that Habré and his allies are represented by Blue game pieces while Goukouni and his allies are Red.

When it comes to conventional troops, just remember these alliances: Green are allied with Goukouni , while Purple and White are allied with the Government Player, which, at the beginning of the game, is Habré.

If you take a quick look at the game pieces and decipher them using the key displayed above, you may begin to notice a few of the broad traits that characterize different armies and commanders involved. A few of these general characteristics are listed here:

  • Lybian forces are characterized by an abundance of tanks, trucks, and large infantry units with excellent conventional battle strength and movement speed.
  • FANT are not the most capable units, even in conventional warfare.
  • French troops, although they are conventional, can be surprisingly capable at guerrilla warfare as well. Some players find that this combined skill makes the French ideal for guarding the capital city!
  • Habré is a much more able military commander compared to Goukouni. His skill, however, only benefits those units with whom he is physically present.

# Game Map

An updated version of the game map is provided with this Handbook. The game rules included in the Handbook are compatible with both the updated map and the original map.

A few of differences between the original 1991 game map and the updated map are explained here:

  • A number of charts on the original map can be found on a secondary reference sheet included with the updated map. The map legend is also included on this sheet.
  • In order to enter a sanctuary on the updated map, units simply enter the sanctuary country, 2.5 on the map legend. The updated map does not include the dedicated sanctuary boxes seen on the old map.
  • The dedicated sanctuary boxes on the legacy map include a New Arrivals section. On the updated map, the sanctuary entry hexes 2.4 serve the purpose of a New Arrivals section. In other words, the entry hexes do not merely indicate where units can enter, but they are also where the units should be placed upon arrival. Once the units are no longer New Arrivals, they are moved into the sanctuary country proper 2.5.
  • The visual elements on the updates map legend are numbered so that they can be more eaily referenced from the game rules. Ignore these references when using the legacy map.

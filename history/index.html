# Land Cruiser J70 series

In the 1980s there were three major Land Cruiser lineups: the J40 series, the J60 series, and the J70 series. The principal difference between these lineups is that the J40 and J70 series were more rugged variants; the J60 was a more comfortable variant. While J40 was an older vehicle lineup which originated in 1960, the J70 went into production beginning in 1984.

While all models of Land Cruiser and Hilux became popular for low-budget warfare in undeveloped countries, the long-wheelbase J75 and 77 — called the J78 post-1999 — were especially favored for both their ruggedness and their interior layout, the the J77/78 even being dubbed "the Troop Carrier," or "Troopy." Additionally, the J79 pickup variant was ideal for use as a mechanized vehicle in desert warfare.

Despite the Chadian military's gradual acquisition of heavier armored vehicles over the decades following the Toyota War, the J70 remained an indespensable vehicle for the military. During the Boko Haram was in the 2010s, the J70 series — and espcially the J79 — can be spotted in nearly every photo of the Chadian military containing vehicles other than tanks.
